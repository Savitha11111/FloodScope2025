## Assam Flood Situation on June 8, 2025

Assam is currently experiencing significant flooding as of June 8, 2025. While the overall flood situation has improved marginally in recent days due to receding river levels and reduced rainfall, a large number of people remain affected:

- **Affected Population:** Over 3.37 lakh (337,000) people are still impacted by floods across 12 districts, with the worst-hit areas being Sribhumi, Hailakandi, and Cachar[1][4][6].
- **Displacement and Relief:** More than 36,000 people are taking shelter in 133 relief camps, and extensive relief operations are ongoing[1][2][6].
- **Casualties:** The death toll from floods and landslides this season has reached at least 23 in Assam, with additional casualties reported across the Northeast[1][4][6].
- **Rivers:** Major rivers, including the Brahmaputra, Barak, Kopili, and Kushiyara, are still flowing above danger levels in several locations, although water levels are generally receding[1][2][4].
- **Crops and Wildlife:** Over 12,000 hectares of cropland remain inundated, and Kaziranga National Park has also been affected, with some animal casualties reported[1][4][6].

### Key Districts Affected
- **Sribhumi:** Over 1.93 lakh people affected
- **Hailakandi:** Around 74,000 people affected
- **Cachar:** Approximately 56,000 people affected[1][4][6]

### Conclusion

Yes, there is an ongoing flood situation in Assam as of June 8, 2025. Although conditions are improving and water levels are receding, large areas remain inundated, and many people are still displaced or affected by the floods[1][2][4][6].
